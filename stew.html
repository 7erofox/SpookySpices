Cuddle Stew

Setting

This stew tastes like a cuddle. It's what I crave when I'm sick, sad or tired. It comes together really quickly, uses relatively cheap ingredients, and you don't need to babysit it.

    Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon of coconut oil
  • 2 teaspoons of turmeric
  • 2 teaspoons of cumin
  • 2 teaspoons of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • Chilli flakes, salt, pepper
  • 1 Bulb of Garlic
  • 1 Chunk of Ginger
  • 2 Sweet potatoes
  • 1 Leek
  • 2 Stalks of Celery
  • 1 Cup of Red lentils
  • 1 Can of Coconut Milk
  • Handful of Cruciferous Vegetables

Instructions

Blitz garlic and ginger, add to a pot with all spices, and oil.

Cook on a medium heat until fragrant.

Blitz up leek and celery, and add these too, stirring to coat evenly for 3-5 minutes.

Add lentils and blitzed sweet potatoes, coat in oil and spices, then add salt, pepper and chilli flakes.

Cover with water and bring to a boil. At this point you are welcome to add stock cube(s), but we find we often don't need to.

Lower to a simmer and ignore until sweet potatoes and lentils are fully cooked - about 25 minutes.

Add coconut milk and cruciferous vegetables - we typically add chard or kale since this is what we often have growing, but broccoli, cabbage, cauliflower and brussels sprouts work great too - and simmer for another five minutes.

Top with seasonal fresh herbs, and enjoy with a huge chunk of bread, oatgurt, or whatever else you might want.
